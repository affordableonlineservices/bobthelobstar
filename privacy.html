This Privacy Policy describes how BARILLAS AI LLC ("BoB The Lobstar AI," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects your information when you use our website bobthelobstar.com and our AI assistant service (collectively, the "Service"). By using the Service, you agree to the practices described in this Policy.

If you do not agree with this Policy, please do not use the Service.

BARILLAS AI LLC is a limited liability company organized under the laws of Arizona, United States, with its principal place of business in Phoenix, Arizona.

Contact for privacy matters: Email: aibob@bobthelobstar.com Mail: BARILLAS AI LLC, Phoenix, Arizona

For the purposes of EU data protection law, BARILLAS AI LLC is the "data controller" of your personal information.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of information:

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account information: email address, chosen username, and password. Passwords are stored using industry-standard hashing (bcrypt) and are never viewable by us in plaintext.
  • Conversation content: the messages you send to BoB, the responses BoB generates, and any business context, preferences, files, or personal information you voluntarily include in conversations. This is what powers BoB's persistent memory feature.
  • Payment information: when you subscribe, payment is processed by our payment provider (Stripe, Inc.). We do not receive or store your full credit or debit card number. We receive and store limited information such as your subscription status, the last four digits of your payment card, billing zip code, and billing cycle.
  • Communications with us: any email, support request, or other communication you send us.

2.2 Information We Collect Automatically

  • Usage data: login timestamps, features used, session duration, pages visited, and other product-interaction data.
  • Device and log data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device identifiers, referring URL, and similar technical data.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: see Section 5 (Cookies and Tracking Technologies) below.

2.3 Information From Third Parties

  • Analytics and advertising partners: we use conversion-tracking pixels and SDKs from third parties (including the TikTok Events API and the Meta / Facebook Pixel) that may receive information about your interactions with our website and our ads, including events such as page views, signups, and subscriptions. These partners may use cookies and similar identifiers to associate your activity on our site with their broader tracking network.
  • Payment processor: Stripe returns data about the status of your payment and subscription to us.

2.4 Information from our Facebook Messenger Chatbot

We operate an automated chatbot on our Facebook Page ("BoB The Lobstar AI") that answers questions about our Service using content from this website. When you message the Page:

  • Page-scoped user ID: a numeric identifier Facebook assigns that uniquely identifies you to our Page only (it is not your public Facebook ID and cannot be used to identify you on other Facebook apps). We need this value to route our reply back to you.
  • Message content: the text of messages you send, plus a short rolling window of the most recent turns of our conversation.
  • Message metadata: message timestamps and message IDs assigned by Meta.

Through the Messenger integration we do not collect your name, email address, profile photo, friends list, or any other Facebook profile information.

Messages received through Messenger are held only in server memory (RAM) as a short conversation history. They are not written to any database or long-term storage. All in-memory conversation state is automatically cleared whenever our server restarts (typically multiple times per week). A copy of the message is transmitted to our AI model provider (Groq, Inc.) in order to generate a reply; see Section 4.1.

2.5 Information We Do Not Collect

We do not knowingly collect:

  • Sensitive personal information such as Social Security numbers, government identification numbers, biometric data, or precise geolocation.
  • Information from individuals under the age of 18.

If you believe you have provided sensitive information by accident, please contact us and we will delete it.

  • Payment and billing records: retained for up to seven (7) years to comply with tax, accounting, and anti-fraud obligations.
  • Logs and security records: retained for up to 24 months for security and abuse-prevention purposes.
  • Backup copies: may persist for a reasonable additional period before being overwritten.
  • Legal holds: we may retain information longer if required by law or relevant to an actual or anticipated legal matter.

After the applicable retention period, we delete, anonymize, or aggregate the information so that it can no longer be associated with you.

7.1 Facebook Messenger Data Deletion

Because our Facebook Messenger chatbot only stores messages in server memory (RAM) and automatically evicts older turns, message data is effectively self-deleting within a short window of your last message. To proactively request that we purge any lingering conversation data associated with your Facebook Page-scoped user ID:

  1. Email aibob@bobthelobstar.com with the subject line "Messenger Data Deletion".
  2. Include (a) the name of the Facebook Page you messaged ("BoB The Lobstar AI") and (b) an approximate date range of your conversation. You do not need to send us your Page-scoped user ID; we will match it on our side.
  3. We will confirm deletion within 30 days.

You may also unlink our Page from your Facebook account at any time by visiting Facebook's Settings → Apps and Websites. After unlinking, Facebook will stop forwarding your messages to us.

California residents have additional rights under the CCPA, including the right to know the specific pieces of personal information collected, the categories of sources, the business purpose for collecting it, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it. You may designate an authorized agent to make a request on your behalf.

EU, UK, and Swiss residents have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe we have violated applicable law.

We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.
