Cookie Policy

Effective Date: April 18, 2026 Last Updated: April 18, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how BARILLAS AI LLC ("BoB The Lobstar AI," "we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website bobthelobstar.com and in connection with our AI assistant service (the "Service"). It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

By using the Service, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this Policy. Where required by law, we will obtain your consent through a cookie banner before setting non-essential cookies.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, phone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognize your device and store certain information about your activity. Other technologies that work similarly to cookies — including pixels, local storage, and software development kits (SDKs) — are treated the same way under this Policy.

Cookies can be:

  • First-party: set directly by the website you are visiting (bobthelobstar.com).
  • Third-party: set by other organizations whose services are embedded in the website (such as analytics or advertising providers).
  • Session cookies: deleted automatically when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • To operate the Service — including signing you in, keeping you logged in, and securing your account.
  • To remember your preferences — such as language or UI preferences.
  • To understand how the Service is used — so we can improve features and diagnose problems.
  • To measure the effectiveness of our advertising — so we can tell whether the ads we run are reaching the right people and bringing qualified users to the Service.

3. Cookies and Trackers We Use

The following table summarizes the cookies, pixels, and similar technologies we currently use. The specific names and expiration periods may change from time to time; we will update this Policy to reflect material changes.

3.1 Strictly Necessary (First-Party)

These are required for the Service to function. You cannot opt out of these without losing the ability to use the Service.

Tracker Type Purpose Expiration
session (Flask session cookie) First-party, HTTP-only Maintains your signed-in session so you can access your account and conversation history. Session (cleared when you log out or close the browser)
CSRF / security tokens First-party Protects against cross-site request forgery attacks. Session

3.2 Advertising and Conversion Tracking (Third-Party)

These are used to measure the performance of our advertising on third-party platforms and to optimize future campaigns. They may set cookies under the domains listed.

Tracker Provider Domains Purpose Expiration
TikTok Pixel / TikTok Events API TikTok / ByteDance (TikTok for Business) tiktok.com, tiktokw.us, analytics.tiktok.com Measures conversions (page views, signups, subscriptions) resulting from TikTok advertising; helps optimize ad delivery. Typically up to 13 months
Meta Pixel (Facebook Pixel) Meta Platforms, Inc. facebook.com, www.facebook.com, connect.facebook.net Measures conversions resulting from Facebook and Instagram advertising; helps optimize ad delivery and build custom audiences for future campaigns. Typically up to 90 days (_fbp), with session identifiers (fbc) up to 90 days

These third parties may combine the information they collect on our site with information they collect from other websites and services to build user profiles for their own advertising and analytics purposes. For details, see their respective privacy policies:

  • TikTok: https://www.tiktok.com/legal/privacy-policy
  • Meta: https://www.facebook.com/privacy/policy/

3.3 Payment Processing (Third-Party)

Tracker Provider Purpose
Stripe cookies and JavaScript Stripe, Inc. Enables secure payment processing and fraud prevention on our payment forms.

Stripe's own cookie and privacy practices are described at https://stripe.com/privacy.

4. Your Choices

You can control cookies in several ways:

4.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers let you view, delete, and block cookies. Common browser controls can be found here:

  • Chrome: https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647
  • Firefox: https://support.mozilla.org/kb/cookies-information-websites-store-on-your-computer
  • Safari (macOS): https://support.apple.com/guide/safari/manage-cookies-sfri11471/mac
  • Safari (iOS): https://support.apple.com/HT201265
  • Edge: https://support.microsoft.com/microsoft-edge/delete-cookies-in-microsoft-edge-63947406-40ac-c3b8-57b9-2a946a29ae09

Blocking strictly necessary cookies will prevent you from signing in or using the Service.

4.2 Cookie Banner

Where required by applicable law (for example, for users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, and California), we present a cookie banner that allows you to accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the cookie preferences link in our website footer.

4.3 Advertising Opt-Outs

You can limit interest-based advertising from participating companies through the following industry tools:

  • Digital Advertising Alliance: https://optout.aboutads.info
  • Network Advertising Initiative: https://optout.networkadvertising.org
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance: https://www.youronlinechoices.eu

You can also opt out of personalized advertising directly on the platforms themselves:

  • TikTok ad settings: available in the TikTok app under Settings → Privacy → Ads.
  • Meta ad settings: https://www.facebook.com/adpreferences

4.4 Mobile Device Opt-Outs

Mobile operating systems provide their own controls for limiting ad tracking:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → disable "Allow Apps to Request to Track."
  • Android: Settings → Privacy → Ads → enable "Opt out of Ads Personalization."

4.5 Do Not Track

Our website does not respond to "Do Not Track" ("DNT") browser signals because no common industry standard exists for interpreting them. You can still use the controls described above.

5. Effect of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies:

  • You may not be able to sign in to or use the Service (strictly necessary cookies).
  • You may still see our advertising, but it will be less relevant to you.
  • Our ability to measure the performance of our advertising will be reduced, which may mean we invest less in reaching users in your region or on particular platforms.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time as our use of cookies evolves or as laws change. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date and, where appropriate, notify you through the Service or via a refreshed cookie banner.

7. Contact Us

Questions about our use of cookies?
